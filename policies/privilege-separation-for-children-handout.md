# Privilege Separation for Children  
A One‑Page Parent Guide

Children use standard (non‑administrator) accounts on household computers, even when they own the device. This protects the system, the child’s work, and the household’s data.

---

## Why Children Do Not Receive Administrator Access

Administrator privileges allow a person to:

1. install or remove software  
1. change system settings  
1. bypass security controls  
1. access all user data  
1. modify or damage the operating system  

These actions require adult judgment.

**Administrator privileges bypass all parental controls.**  
Only adults may use administrator accounts.

---

## Why Children Use Standard Accounts

Standard accounts protect:

1. the operating system  
1. the child’s files  
1. the household’s data  
1. the stability of the computer  

Children can explore and learn without risking system damage.

---

## Password Safety Rules

1. Children may not observe an adult typing the administrator password.  
1. Children may not provide the administrator password during installation or setup.  
1. The administrator password must remain private to adults.

---

## Supervised Learning

Children may participate in administrative tasks **only under direct adult supervision**, such as:

1. installing the operating system  
1. performing updates  
1. installing development tools  

During supervised sessions:

1. the child may type commands  
1. the child must pause before pressing ENTER  
1. an adult must confirm each action  

---

## After Supervised Sessions

1. the child returns to their standard account  
1. the administrator password remains private  
1. the child cannot elevate privileges  

---

## Summary

1. Children use standard accounts for safety and stability.  
1. Adults hold administrator privileges.  
1. Administrator access bypasses all parental controls.  
1. Children may not see or enter the administrator password.  
1. Supervised learning is encouraged and safe.  
1. After supervision, children return to standard accounts.

This policy protects the computer and supports a safe learning environment for children.
